Lesson 5: Rounding Without Memorizing Rules

Goal: Round numbers using landmarks (not “5 or more…” panic).

Hook

Rounding is choosing the nearest “landmark.” You’re not following a chant — you’re choosing the closest target.

The Hack

Landmark method:

  1. Circle the place you’re rounding to (the landmark place).
  2. Look one digit to the right (the decider).
  3. Decider 0–4 → stay. Decider 5–9 → bump up.
  4. Everything to the right becomes zeros.

Standard Method

To round to a place value, check the digit to the right. If it is 5 or greater, increase the rounding digit by 1; otherwise keep it the same.

Guided Practice

  1. Round 4,783 to the nearest hundred.
  2. Round 9,949 to the nearest thousand.
  3. Round 1,250 to the nearest hundred.
  4. Round 506 to the nearest ten.

Boss Problem

Two students round 3,450 to the nearest hundred and get different answers. One says 3,400. One says 3,500. Who is right? Explain.

Exit Check

Round 6,501 to the nearest thousand.

Answer

7,000 (decider is 5)
